[ SPARKRAT ]

Scientific Name:Destructivus captivus

SPARKRAT

Discovered by / at: Ishita, Zone 77

Waste management drones during a hazardous material sweep of Zone-77

Family:Rattus electrus

Last surviving specimen of radiation-mutated Rattus electrus, hybridized with junkyard machinery.

Habitat: Electric Junkyard

Confined to electrified cages in radioactive e-waste junkyards.

Appearance:

Circular body with exposed wiring instead of fur Rusted metal color of body No legs, hovers over the ground in the cage Super angry and frustrated Face fused with a broken power cable

Key Characteristics:

  • Self-Destructive Loop: Obsessively attempts escape despite knowing the cage’s lethal voltage
  • Cheese Dependency: Only calms when fed dairy – its organic components still crave calcium
  • Electric Addiction: Drawn to zap itself on cage walls to charge deteriorating power cells
  • ...

What we know so far about SPARKRAT? The Sparkrat’s flesh regenerates nightly but its mind degrades daily. Cheese briefly restores mammalian instincts, slowing its suicide attempts. Scanners show its heart is 63% gearbox.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature Collect cheese from scavenged lunchboxes in the junkyard Click mouse to draw it towards the cheese and prevent electrocution Throws to interrupt its shock cycles Balance feeding vs. its growing voltage resistance
